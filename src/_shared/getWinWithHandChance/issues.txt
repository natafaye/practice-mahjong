1. Probability Summation Error (Non-Disjoint Events)
The function sums the probabilities of all possible joker combinations (drawingChance += jokerComboChance). However, these
combinations are not mutually exclusive. For example, if you need a 1-Dot and have one Joker available in the wall:
  * Combo A: Draw the 1-Dot naturally.
  * Combo B: Draw the Joker to substitute for the 1-Dot.

Drawing both the 1-Dot and the Joker satisfies both combinations. Summing their probabilities double-counts these overlapping        
outcomes, which overestimates the chance and can lead to a drawingChance greater than 1.

2. Conservative "Exactly" Logic
The multivariate hypergeometric formula implementation calculates the probability of drawing exactly the number of tiles specified in
a combo and zero extra of any tiles listed in totalAvailable.

  1 jokerComboChance *= nCr(totalUnseenTiles - totalAvailable, thisPlayerTurnsLeft - totalNeeded);
In Mahjong, drawing more than the required number of a needed tile is still a win. By excluding outcomes where extra winning tiles   
are drawn, the function significantly underestimates the probability of each individual combination.

4. nCr Precision and Overflow
The nCr function is susceptible to precision loss:
  * Precision: Intermediate products (like res * (n - i + 1)) can easily exceed Number.MAX_SAFE_INTEGER ($2^{53} - 1$), causing the   
    result to become a floating-point approximation.
  * Performance: The division jokerComboChance /= nCr(totalUnseenTiles, thisPlayerTurnsLeft) is performed inside the loop. Dividing by
    a massive number ($7.23 \times 10^{26}$ for $133C25$) repeatedly can lead to underflow errors or unnecessary precision loss.      

Recommendations
  * Use Complementary Probability: Calculate the probability of not winning (failing all combos) and subtract from 1, though this is  
    complex with overlapping requirements.
  * Simplify the Estimate: For a "reasonable estimate," consider using a simpler model that calculates the probability of drawing at  
    least the required number of tiles for the "best" combination, rather than summing all combinations.
  * Move Huge Divisions: Calculate the denominator nCr(totalUnseenTiles, thisPlayerTurnsLeft) once outside the loop.
